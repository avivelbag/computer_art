# Piece 39 — Apollonian Gasket

An Apollonian circle packing generated by recursive application of Descartes' Circle
Theorem. Three equal circles packed inside a bounding circle seed the construction;
each triangular interstice is filled with its unique inscribed circle, producing a
fractal with Hausdorff dimension ≈ 1.3057.

## Technique

1. **Initial configuration**: one enclosing circle of radius R (curvature k = −1/R) and
   three equal inner circles of radius r = R√3/(2+√3), arranged at 90°, 210°, 330°.
   All four circles satisfy Descartes' Theorem:
   `(k₁+k₂+k₃+k₄)² = 2(k₁²+k₂²+k₃²+k₄²)`

2. **Apollonian reflection formula** — given four mutually tangent circles, the twin of
   circle C_p tangent to triple (Ca, Cb, Cc) is found without a square root:
   ```
   k_new = 2(ka + kb + kc) − k_parent
   z_new = (2(za·ka + zb·kb + zc·kc) − z_parent·k_parent) / k_new
   ```
   This avoids the sign ambiguity of the full Descartes formula and is numerically stable.

3. **Recursion**: BFS over tangent triples until the inscribed circle radius < 2 px.

4. **Palette**: six stops mapped via log(r_max/r) / log(r_max/2) — deep teal (outermost,
   largest circles) to warm cream (innermost, smallest), making fractal depth visible.
