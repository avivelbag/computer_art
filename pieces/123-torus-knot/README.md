# Piece 123 — Torus Knot Curves

A (p,q) torus knot is a parametric space curve that wraps p times around the
longitude of a torus and q times around its meridian. Classic knots include the
trefoil (2,3), cinquefoil (2,5), star (3,5), and septafoil (2,7).

## Technique

Parametric equations on a torus of major radius R=200, minor radius r=70:

```
x(t) = (R + r·cos(q·t)) · cos(p·t)
y(t) = (R + r·cos(q·t)) · sin(p·t)
z(t) = r · sin(q·t)
```

A slight x-axis tilt (0.45 rad) gives the projected z coordinate meaningful
depth information. Stroke width is proportional to (z + r) / (2r), so the
z = r·sin(q·t) variation produces a sinusoidal thickness along the curve that
fakes near/far occlusion without any filled 3D shading.

## Animation

The curve is drawn stroke-by-stroke at ~3 segments per frame (60 fps), tracing
the full knot in about 4 seconds before clearing and looping seamlessly.

## Controls

The **Next Knot** button cycles through four classic knots:
- Trefoil (2,3) — 3-lobed, 3 crossings
- Cinquefoil (2,5) — 5-lobed, 5 crossings
- Star (3,5) — 5-pointed star, 10 crossings
- Septafoil (2,7) — 7-lobed, 7 crossings

## Palette

Dark background (`#0d0a14`) with three warm accent colors assigned by depth
band: rose (`#c8405a`) for far segments, amber (`#e88c1a`) for mid, and gold
(`#f5c830`) for near.
