Ising Model — The Temperature of Order

Spins as Magnetic Dipoles

Each cell represents an atom whose electrons give it a tiny magnetic moment called a spin, pointing either up (+1, warm linen) or down (−1, deep indigo). In a ferromagnet neighbouring spins prefer to align: parallel pairs lower the total energy by exchange coupling J. The Hamiltonian is H = −J Σ sᵢsⱼ over all nearest-neighbour pairs.

The Boltzmann Factor

The Metropolis–Hastings algorithm governs thermal fluctuations. Each animation frame N² candidate flips are attempted at randomly chosen sites. A flip that lowers energy (ΔE ≤ 0) is always accepted. A flip that raises energy by ΔE is accepted with probability exp(−ΔE / kT) — the Boltzmann factor.

High temperature makes unfavourable flips likely, dissolving order. Low temperature makes them vanishingly rare, locking spins into aligned domains. In a 2D square lattice the neighbour sum can only be −4, −2, 0, 2, or 4, so only two positive ΔE values arise (4J and 8J) — only two lookup values are needed per frame.

The Curie Temperature & Phase Transition

Below Tc spontaneous magnetisation appears: large aligned domains of one colour dominate and |M| → 1. Above Tc thermal noise overwhelms the coupling and the lattice becomes disordered (paramagnetic), |M| → 0.

Right at Tc the system is scale-free: domains of every size coexist in fractal "critical opalescence." Drag the slider through Tc and watch the magnetisation sparkline collapse or recover. A defect injected below Tc into a large domain quickly heals; above Tc it spreads freely.

Onsager's Exact Solution (1944)

Lars Onsager derived the exact free energy of the 2D square-lattice Ising model — a landmark of mathematical physics. The critical temperature in units J/k is:

Tc = 2 / ln(1 + √2) ≈ 2.2692

The spontaneous magnetisation below Tc obeys M ~ (1 − sinh⁻⁴(2J/kT))^(1/8). The heat capacity diverges logarithmically at Tc — a genuine phase transition with critical exponent α = 0. No closed-form exact solution exists in 3D; the 3D critical temperature must be found numerically.

Domain Formation

Below Tc domains nucleate and coarsen over time: spins form large same-sign islands because each domain-wall bond costs energy 2J. The system slowly minimises wall area. Above Tc domain walls proliferate freely — the cost is overwhelmed by the entropy gained from disorder. The crossover is sharp: the correlation length diverges as ξ ~ |T − Tc|^(−ν) with ν = 1 in 2D, meaning fluctuations of every scale appear exactly at Tc.
