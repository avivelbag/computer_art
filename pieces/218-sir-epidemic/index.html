The SIR Model

Every individual belongs to one of three compartments: Susceptible (can catch the disease), Infected (currently sick and contagious), or Recovered (immune). The dots in the left panel show this state in colour.

Differential Equations

The compartment sizes evolve according to:

dS/dt = −β · S · I / N
dI/dt = β · S · I / N − γ · I
dR/dt = γ · I

β is the transmission rate, γ is the recovery rate, N = S + I + R is the total population. The three values always sum to N — no one is created or destroyed.

Reproduction Number R₀

4.0
R₀ = β / γ

R₀ is the average number of people one infected person infects in a fully susceptible population. When R₀ < 1, each case causes less than one new case — the epidemic shrinks and dies out. When R₀ > 1 it grows.

Flatten the Curve

Reduce β (social distancing, masks) and the red peak in the right panel spreads out and drops in height — the same epidemic, but stretched over a longer time. This buys capacity for health systems.

The Vaccination slider starts a fraction of the population already in the Recovered state. Once enough are vaccinated, the effective R₀ falls below 1 even with a high β — this is herd immunity.
