# SDF Raymarcher — Infinite Folded Space

Sphere-tracing (raymarching) casts one ray per pixel from the camera into the scene, advancing the ray by the value of a signed-distance function (SDF) at each step — because the SDF guarantees a safe step size, the ray converges to the nearest surface in at most 64 iterations without over-stepping. The scene SDF is a single torus (`sdTorus`) repeated infinitely in all directions via *domain repetition*: `mod(p, 2.0) − 1.0` folds all of 3D space into a 2×2×2 cell, so one torus equation becomes an infinite field. Lighting is computed from the finite-difference surface normal, a Phong specular highlight, a 5-step ambient-occlusion approximation, and a distance-based exponential fog that blends surfaces into the deep indigo sky as they recede.

What you are watching: an infinite grid of golden tori floating in deep indigo fog, viewed from a slowly orbiting camera on a 20-second loop. The `uTime` uniform drives both the camera orbit angle and a gentle vertical oscillation, keeping the animation perfectly seamless. Ambient occlusion darkens the concave inner curves of each torus, specular highlights pick out the outer curvature, and a cool blue-violet rim light separates the surfaces from the fog.
