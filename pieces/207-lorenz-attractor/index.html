The Lorenz Attractor

In 1963 Edward Lorenz derived three equations to model warm air rising in convection rolls (the same process that drives thunderstorms). The variables x, y, z represent the rotation rate and temperature gradient of the roll. The system is deterministic — given a starting point the future is fixed — yet chaotic: two trajectories starting a hair's breadth apart diverge exponentially. The teal path begins just ε = 10⁻⁴ away from the amber path; watch how quickly they separate. This is why Lorenz called it the butterfly effect: a butterfly flapping its wings in Brazil might set off a tornado in Texas. The equations are integrated using fourth-order Runge-Kutta (RK4) at dt = 0.005.
