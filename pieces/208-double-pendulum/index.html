Double Pendulum — Lagrangian Mechanics & Chaos

A double pendulum is two rigid arms pivoting in series: the second bob hangs from the tip of the first. The Lagrangian L = T − V (kinetic minus potential energy) yields two coupled nonlinear ODEs for the angles θ₁ and θ₂. Because the equations are nonlinear and the system has two degrees of freedom, its motion is chaotic for most large-angle initial conditions.

The physics is integrated with 4th-order Runge-Kutta (RK4): within each time step the derivative is estimated at four internal points and combined with weights ⅙, ⅓, ⅓, ⅙ — far more accurate than a naïve Euler step and with no small-angle approximation.

The Lyapunov exponent λ quantifies exponential trajectory separation: if the distance between two nearby orbits grows as e^(λt), then λ is the Lyapunov exponent. For the double pendulum λ ≈ 3–7 s⁻¹ depending on initial conditions.
