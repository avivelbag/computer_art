# Piece 38 — No Pattern Repeats

A Penrose P3 tiling (thick and thin rhombi) generated by substitution deflation.
Starting from ten acute Robinson triangles in a decagonal sun, six generations of
golden-ratio deflation produce 2,330 triangles that tile the plane aperiodically —
no translational repetition is possible, yet five-fold rotational symmetry appears
throughout.

## Technique

1. **Initial sun**: 10 acute (36°-72°-72°) triangles share a common apex at the origin,
   outer vertices forming a regular decagon.
2. **Deflation rules** (φ = (1+√5)/2 ≈ 1.618):
   - Acute triangle → 1 acute + 1 obtuse (P placed at 1/φ along one leg)
   - Obtuse triangle → 2 obtuse + 1 acute (Q, R placed at 1/φ along two edges)
3. **Rhombi**: pairs of acute triangles form thick rhombi (72°/108° angles); pairs of
   obtuse triangles form thin rhombi (36°/144° angles). The ratio thick:thin → φ.
4. **SVG**: polygons grouped by type; 1-decimal coordinate precision. No JavaScript.
